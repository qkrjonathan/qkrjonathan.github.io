Introduction

Back to top

At this point I've probably mentioned Exabeam, the Security Information and Event Management software (SIEM) I had the opportunity to test out and also present to the IT team during my internship, more than a few times in this blog. So it's become fairly clear to me, and most likely you, dear reader, that it's high time that I start properly committing to making SIEMs and log analysis and alert remediation etc. etc. become my thing.

How I got from there to deciding to pursue the Splunk Core Certified User certification is honestly pure chance. This word, "Splunk," had been planted into my head from having seen it constantly in the qualifications sections of job postings. So when my YouTube algorithm threw a little video about Splunk to see what would happen, like a grandmother in Central Park tossing breadcrumbs at pigeons, I happily took the bait.

Anyways, internet pipelines are a dangerous thing, and now I've committed to getting this certification and proving myself as a competent basic user of Splunk — and I assume to some extent most SIEMs.

Timeline


14 July 2025

Back to top

I have to give it to Tech with Jono with getting me started on this. His video on installing Splunk and downloading practice data for Splunk was that video that YouTube fed to me to see if I was interested. And indeed I was.

While I was setting up my homelab, I'd come across the idea a few times of getting a SIEM onto that homelab and learning how it works. But the lingering question in my mind had been, How do you practice log analysis and alert triaging or whatever on a SIEM when your network has literally nothing going on? The concept of practice data didn't even exist in my mind for some reason until this video.

To be clear, I wasn't following the actual instructions in that video step by step from the getgo. But it definitely sparked something.

That led to this video by CYBERWOX on how to pass the Splunk Core Certified User exam. This is how I found out such an exam existed.

Until this moment I'd had two distinct ideas in my head: (a) that I wanted to get into learning Splunk and having fun with it, and (b) I also wanted to have some kind of formal way of proving the experience I'd get along the way to put on my resume. Learning about the SCCU was when those ideas finally merged together.

Yet another source of affirmation was this video by TYTE about how she passed the exam after 10 days of studying. Now this was incredibly encouraging. Both of my certs so far (CCNA and Security+) took months. But two weeks?

Splunk's official test blueprint for the SCCU offers a "suggested and non-exhaustive" list of eight free training courses that cover the exam material:

  • Intro to Splunk
  • Using Fields
  • Scheduling Reports and Alerts
  • Visualizations
  • Working with Time
  • Statistical Processing
  • Leveraging Lookups and Subsearches
  • Search Optimization

I signed up for the Intro to Splunk course and got started. In the meantime, now following Tech with Jono's video from earlier (albeit using my Kali VM as the OS, not Windows as Jono did in the video), I downloaded the free trial of Splunk Enterprise as well as the Splunk Universal Forwarder. I got them as .deb files and unpackaged with them with sudo dpkg -i [filename].
